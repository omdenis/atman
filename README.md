# atman

*An operating system for the self — life as a system you tend, not a future you chase.*

**atman** is a personal-development methodology realized as a plain-text, git-versioned
Obsidian vault. It replaces thinking in *states*, *projects*, and *someday-futures* with one
discipline: **run the system, improve it by +1 each block, and let knowing arrive through
experience — not theory.**

Everything you decide, observe, or learn is captured *immediately* to a file. Nothing lives
only in your head or in an AI's memory. The vault is the source of truth; the agents are
just hands.

## The shift in mindset
- **From states → to a system.** Stop managing moods and milestones; tend one living system.
- **From outcomes → to +1.** Each work-block leaves the system one increment better —
  compounding beats chasing.
- **From living-in-the-future → to the present log.** The plan is a *projection* of what's
  true now, rebuildable at any moment.
- **From borrowed theory → to experiential knowing.** Principles are earned from logged
  experience, not copied from books.

## Core principles
- **Event sourcing.** An append-only log of events and decisions is the single source of
  truth; every dashboard, plan, and decision-matrix is a *projection* rebuilt from it. Events
  are never edited — a correction is a new event that `supersedes` the old one.
- **Immediate capture.** *"Not written to a file = does not exist."* Agent memory (SQLite,
  MEMORY.md) is a **cache** that can be rebuilt from the vault.
- **Calibrated decisions.** Every decision carries an `expected_outcome`, a `confidence`
  (1–10), and a `review_at` date — a forecast with feedback, not just a choice.
- **Decisions carry their context.** A significant artifact is the *result* of a recorded
  decision (the problem, the alternatives rejected, the why) and links back to it —
  provenance is built into the model, so you can always trace where the legs grow from.
- **Self-developing.** The methodology develops itself inside `systems/atman/` — literally
  the machinery that builds the machinery: a system that improves the rules by which it
  improves.
- **Ritual at the threshold (sandhya).** Each work-block is opened and closed deliberately —
  read the latest handoff, frame, act; then synthesize the block into a review and write a
  new handoff that ends mid-stride (a "Hemingway bridge"), so the next session starts
  cold-free.

## Why "atman"
*Ātman* (Sanskrit) is the higher Self — the unchanging witness behind the bustle of daily
life. The name doesn't describe the contents (those age); it names *who lives here*. Where an
*avatar* is the higher descending into a form — a projection outward — **atman is the source
those projections come from.** The vault is that source.

**Atman - the return to your true self**

There was a moment when I wanted to bring order not just to my tasks, but to my entire life. Not another list of goals - you can keep generating those forever, and they prove nothing. I wanted something else: to see what actually works for me. Not "I'm planning to take care of my health," but "here is my health system, and here is the state it is in right now." Not dreams, but the things that are actually running.

All that was left was to give this place a name. I tried a few: "Avatar," "Master," "Systems"... but I kept stumbling over the same thing - they had no soul. Those words described a role or a shelf, but they did not touch anything deeper.

And then I found not just a similar word, but the exact one. Atman - in the Indian tradition, the true Self: the selfhood that lies deeper than the ego, roles, and everyday noise, the still observer behind the stream of daily life. It turned out that my own description - "the higher self beyond chores and endless busyness" - was almost a literal translation of this word. Even with "Avatar," I was reaching in the same direction: an avatar is the descent of the higher into form, a projection outward. Atman is the source itself, the place those projections come from. I was pointing in the right direction - I had just grabbed the effect instead of the cause.

Now I have a home - Atman. And I have a ritual at its threshold: I enter it in the morning, and I close the day in the evening. That movement itself feels like a quiet reminder of the place I am living from today: not from hurry, but from the observer.

And when I first said this word out loud, I felt a sense of inner knowing rise in me, a kind of density in the field - as if something deeper than the mental layer had become present. Merging with that larger presence felt like coming home: warmth, silence, wisdom, inner strength.

That is where it all began.

**Atman skills:**

💎 **atman-checkin - सन्ध्य (Sandhyā), the threshold rite**
Sandhyā is the twilight junction between states, the point where the practitioner crosses a boundary consciously. This skill opens a work block: it reads the latest handoff and recent events, frames awareness through `session-open`, and brings you straight to the "bridge" point - without the friction of a blank page. Entering the flow as a ritual of crossing.

💎 **atman-checkout - विसर्जन (Visarjana), the farewell rite**
Visarjana is the closing act of a puja: the energy-event accumulated during the block is not abandoned, but gathered and respectfully released. This skill synthesizes the events of the block into a single projection-overview - it never manually writes "what was done" - and creates a new data handoff, ending with a Hemingway-style bridge: a seed for the next entry.

💎 **atman-state - दर्शन (Darśana), the seeing of what is**
Darśana is an instant, direct seeing of the true form. This skill creates an immutable snapshot of the system's state: first, the AI studies the files, decisions, and events, then it runs an adaptive interrogation - 15+ sparring questions - drawing out knowledge that is not present in the files. What emerges is a rich vision: strengths, pains, blind spots, and the domain's core axes.
